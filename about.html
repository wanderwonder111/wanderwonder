Most academic learning happens within institutional spaces, yet many design programs are expanding these boundaries. Field trips, study trips, workshops, residencies, site-specific projects: there are many ways a classroom can move elsewhere.

wander wonder began with a simple curiosity: How does a change of location affect the way we observe, think, and create? And what can these educational experiments tell us about the future of design education?

We started collecting examples of programs that take place outside traditional classrooms but found the available information to be limited. Rather than collecting general information, we became interested in the experiences behind them. We began reaching out to educators, organizers, and students—conducting conversations over video calls, in person, and through email. With educators, we focused on pedagogy; with students, on the raw experience of working outside the studio.

This website brings together conversations with those who have experienced off-site classes. While our examples are currently drawn from Europe and are limited in scope, they open up a vital space to think about what future design education might look like—and where we might go next.
